This website is an overview of Greek Mythology. From the Gods and Goddesses to the heroes and monsters, if you want to learn anything about Greek Mythology, you've come to the right place.

The Olympians

The Original Six

Zeus

Zeus is the king of all the gods, as well as, the father to many. He is also the god of weather, law, and fate. He is one of the more powerful Olympians, known as the Big Three.

Read More »

Hera

Hera is the queen of the gods, married to Zeus. She is the goddess of women and marriage. It is believed if you are blessed by her, you do not have to worry about relationships.

Read More »

Poseidon

Poseidon is the god of the sea and the protector of all waters. He is often dipicted as wielding a trident or three-progend spear. He is another one of the Big Three.

Read More »

Hades

Hades is the god of the underworld. He is the final of the Big Three and was also called the God of Wealth because he possessed the precious metals of the earth.

Read More »

Demeter

Demeter was the daughter of Cronos and Rhea. She was the goddess of harvest and fertility. Only women attended the Thesmophoria, a fertility festival held in honor of Demeter.

Read More »

Hestia

She was a sibling to Demeter, Hades, Poseidon, Hera, and Zeus. Hestia is completely omitted from the works of Homer, author of the Iliad and the Odyssey.

Read More »

Greek Mythology's Most Known Heroes

Greek Mythology shows a variety of men and women heroes who were of special strength, courage, or ability. They were often of divine ancestry and noted for their superhuman courageous act. Here are a few of the most well known heroes.

Achilles Mind the heel

Achilles, the son of Releus and Thetis, was the greatest of all Greek heroes who took part in the Trojan War. Thetis bathed her child in the waters of the River Styx, knowing her child was destined to become a warrior, making him immortal aside from his heel which would cost him his life.

Read More »

Heracles A symbol of masculinity and bravery

Heracles is one of the most recognized and famous of the heroes in Greek mythology. He was the son of Zeus and Alcmene and is considered the champion of the Olympian order against terrible monsters.

Read More »

Odysseus The Odyssey

Odysseus, the son of the king of Ithaca, Laertes, and Anticlea, was known for his leadership, wit, intelligence as well as his ten-year journey after the Trojan War. This journey was caused after Odysseus angered Poseidon who did whatever he could to keep Odysseus from returning home.

Greek Monsters

Greek Mythology tales would not be the same without its monsters. They live to torment and challenge the lives of the mortals and Gods by being obstacles to heroes or ocassionally coming to their aid.

Chimera

The Chimera was a three-headed beast with a lion's body and head, a tail that ended into a snake's head, and a goat's neck and head from the middle of her back. She could also breathe fire from her mouth.

Read More »

The Gorgons

The Gorgons were three deadly sisters, Stheno, Euryale, and Medusa, whose hair was made of living, venomous snakes and could turn anyone into stone just be looking into their eyes.

Read More »

Typhon

Typhone is the "Father of all Monsters". He was the last child of Gaia, fathered by Tartarus, and is considered the most powerful and deadliest creatures in Greek Mythology.
